2. Core Truth

An AI that evolves without governance will eventually break truth.

STEP 10 ensures that the system can improve itself without violating logic, safety, architecture, or human intent.

3. What Self-Evolution Really Means

  • Improving plans, not inventing goals
  • Optimizing execution paths, not bypassing rules
  • Refining architecture, not mutating it blindly

6. Policy Engine

Policies define what the system is allowed to change and what is immutable. 

Policy Types:
- Architectural invariants
- Security boundaries
- Data privacy rules
- Cost & resource limits

7. Evolution Proposal Flow

Observed Pattern
   ↓
Evolution Proposal
   ↓
Risk Analysis
   ↓
Policy Validation
   ↓
Approve or Reject

8. Risk Analysis

  • Backward compatibility
  • Security exposure
  • Performance regression
  • Knowledge corruption risk

9. Alignment Validation

  • No contradiction with original intent
  • No violation of verified truths
  • No hidden objective creation

10. Change Approval Gate

Changes can be:

  • Auto-approved (low risk, proven pattern)
  • Human-approved (medium risk)
  • Rejected (any contradiction detected)

11. Audit & Traceability

Every change stores:
- Reason
- Data source
- Risk score
- Approval decision
- Rollback path

12. Output

EvolutionDecision {
  change_type: optimization | refactor | reject
  risk_level: low | medium | high
  approved: boolean
}
